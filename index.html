Research landscape

Related work

Seah and Olds (2013)

  • First known work of battery-less multi-hop communication
  • Unconstrained node transmits power and synchronizes devices

Parks, Sample, Zhao, Smith (2013)

  • Harvest RF energy from TV broadcast tower 4km away
  • Achieves a 1Hz sampling rate
  • Sensors nodes connected to sink in star topology

Minami et al (2005)

  • Sensor nodes with solar + ultra-capacitor
  • Developed a multi-hop protocol using wake signals and flooding
  • Due to size of capacitor, sampling periods > 1 minute

    • No known work forms a multi-hop network without an unconstrained node for power or synchronization

Network formation research

Research motivation and challenges

Why form multi-hop networks?

  • Can form networks entirely of resource-constrained nodes
  • Allows for greater spatial coverage at low cost
  • Can serve as a building block within larger heterogeneous information networks

What are the challenges?

  • Extremely low energy budgets rule out protocols with even modest communication and computation overheads
  • Devices duty-cycle based on available energy, creating synchronization challenges
Applications should be resilient to packet-loss and latency

Research questions

  • Can battery-less, intermittent devices sustain a multi-hop network?
  • What network and device parameters drive performance metrics such as average throughput and latency?
  • What is the baseline network performance that can then be improved upon with future protocols
  • For signal processing applications, what are the effective spatial and temporal sampling rates associated with the baseline network?

Research approach

Use simplified device and network model to evaluate network performance

  • Compile a reduced set of parameters to drive model
  • Develop a primitive protocol that supports multi-hop communication and is mathemetically analytical
  • Identify regions of the parameter space with promising performance

Simulate devices and network to validate above analysis

  • Using the established models and protocols, simulate the network?
  • Compare simulated performance to theoretical values

Test analysis against more realistic device models

  • Systemically reduce set of assumptions and reasses validity of model
